Abstract: This study tests a lead and lag hypothesis using U.S. and Japanese sector ETF data.
The hypothesis is that, because trading hours do not overlap, information that is fully reflected
in the market that closes first is then incorporated into the market that opens later, from the
open through the trading day. Specifically, we use same day close to close returns of U.S. sector
ETFs as the information set, and we predict next day open to close returns of Japanese sector
ETFs. To do so, we build a predictive signal based on a subspace regularized PCA applied to the
joint correlation matrix of U.S. and Japanese sectors. The signal can be written as a low rank
linearpredictorofJapanesereturnsfromU.S.returns. Underanidealizedmodelinwhichcommon
factorsarerevealedintheU.S.andspillovertoJapanonthenextday,weshowthatthispredictor
is the best linear forecast. In our empirical analysis, a long short strategy based on the proposed
signal outperforms several baselines, such as momentum, plain PCA without regularization, and a
double sort that combines momentum and the proposed signal. The proposed strategy performs
better in terms of risk adjusted performance and maximum drawdown.
